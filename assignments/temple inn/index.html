Billings, Montana Temple

The temple in Billings, Montana is the 66th operating temple and first built in Montana. To this day, it is the only temple in the state. The temple was announced August 30, 1996 and was dedicated by Gordon B. Hinckley November 20-21, 1999.

The grounds cover 10 acres and are beautifully landscaped. The temple has a georgeous background of 300 foot tall Rimrock cliffs. The temple itself is only 33, 800 square feet, a small temple, relatively speaking, but offering nearly all the ammenities of a larger temple. From the foyer, patrons can look up through a skylight and see the Angel Moroni glistening atop the temple spire.

Billings is Montana's largest city and the temple there serves central and Eastern Montana and the Northwest corner of Wyoming. Many patrons travel three or four hours to worship and serve in the temple.
